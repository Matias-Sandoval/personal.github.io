Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how Rule of Three handles information. Rule of Three is a macOS app designed to quickly calculate proportional values using the classic rule of three formula.

Last updated: May 15, 2026

Summary: Rule of Three works locally on your Mac. The app does not collect, store, sell, track, or share your personal data.

1. Local Calculations

Rule of Three performs calculations directly on your device. The values you enter into the app are used only to calculate the result shown in the app.

These values are not uploaded to any server and are not sent to the developer.

2. Data Collection

Rule of Three does not collect personal information, usage data, analytics, diagnostics, device identifiers, location data, or any other data from your device.

3. App Preferences

Rule of Three may store simple app preferences locally on your Mac, such as appearance mode and decimal precision settings.

These preferences remain on your device and are used only to improve your experience inside the app.

4. Clipboard

If you choose to copy a calculated result, Rule of Three places that result on your Mac clipboard.

The app does not read your clipboard and does not transmit clipboard contents anywhere.

If you choose to contact us through the feedback option, your email app may open a message addressed to us.

Any information you include in that email is provided voluntarily by you and will be used only to read, respond to, and manage your feedback or support request.

Rule of Three does not use third-party analytics, advertising networks, tracking tools, or external data collection services.

7. Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent, trade, or share your personal data with third parties.

8. Children’s Privacy

Rule of Three does not knowingly collect personal information from children or any other users.
